# RNEA in Modern Robotics Twist/Wrench Language

## 1. Brief summary

The **Recursive Newton–Euler Algorithm (RNEA)** computes the inverse dynamics of a robot:

\[
\tau = ID(q, \dot q, \ddot q)
\]

That is, given:

- joint positions \(q\)
- joint velocities \(\dot q\)
- joint accelerations \(\ddot q\)

it computes the required joint torques/forces \(\tau\).

The key idea is to avoid explicitly forming the mass matrix \(M(q)\). Instead, RNEA performs:

1. a **forward pass** to propagate **body twists** and **body accelerations** from root to leaves,
2. a **local rigid-body dynamics step** to compute each link's required **body wrench**,
3. a **backward pass** to propagate **subtree wrenches** from leaves to root and extract joint torques.

In modern robotics language:

- motion lives in the twist space \(se(3)\),
- forces live in the wrench space \(se(3)^*\),
- transforms of twists use \(Ad\),
- transforms of wrenches use \(Ad^*\),
- instantaneous transport of twists uses \(ad\),
- instantaneous transport of wrenches uses \(ad^*\).

---

## 2. Objects and notation

For link \(i\):

- \(V_i \in \mathbb{R}^6\): body twist of link \(i\), expressed in the body frame of link \(i\)
- \(A_i \in \mathbb{R}^6\): body acceleration of link \(i\), expressed in the body frame of link \(i\)
- \(F_i \in \mathbb{R}^6\): body wrench acting on link \(i\)
- \(G_i \in \mathbb{R}^{6\times 6}\): body spatial inertia of link \(i\)
- \(S_i \in \mathbb{R}^6\): **body screw axis** of joint \(i\), expressed in the child/body frame
- \(\lambda(i)\): parent index of body \(i\)
- \(children(i)\): children of body \(i\)

We write twists and wrenches as:

\[
V = \begin{bmatrix} \omega \\ v \end{bmatrix},
\qquad
F = \begin{bmatrix} n \\ f \end{bmatrix}
\]

where:

- \(\omega\): angular velocity
- \(v\): linear velocity of the frame origin
- \(n\): moment
- \(f\): force

---

## 3. Forward pass: body twist recursion

The body twist recursion is:

\[
V_i = Ad_{T_{i,\lambda(i)}} V_{\lambda(i)} + S_i \dot q_i
\]

Interpretation:

- \(Ad_{T_{i,\lambda(i)}} V_{\lambda(i)}\) is the **parent link's twist re-expressed in the child frame**
- \(S_i \dot q_i\) is the **relative joint twist** generated by the motion of joint \(i\)

So the child body twist is:

> inherited parent motion + relative joint motion.

This is the twist-space statement of rigid kinematics.

### Insight

This equation is not about an end-effector specifically. It is about the twist of the **chosen body frame** of link \(i\). If that frame happens to be at an endpoint, then it is the endpoint twist. Otherwise it is just the body-frame twist.

---

## 4. Forward pass: body acceleration recursion

Differentiate the twist recursion in moving body coordinates:

\[
A_i = Ad_{T_{i,\lambda(i)}} A_{\lambda(i)} + S_i \ddot q_i + ad_{V_i}(S_i \dot q_i)
\]

Interpretation:

- \(Ad_{T_{i,\lambda(i)}} A_{\lambda(i)}\): inherited parent acceleration
- \(S_i \ddot q_i\): direct joint acceleration term
- \(ad_{V_i}(S_i \dot q_i)\): convective / Coriolis-like correction due to differentiating a twist in a moving body frame

### Insight

The third term is the 6D analogue of the familiar 3D rotating-frame correction \(\omega \times r\). It is not a different type of object: it is another 6D motion vector. It contributes to the time derivative of a twist, which is why it appears inside the acceleration recursion.

---

## 5. Gravity trick

For a fixed-base robot, physically the base twist is zero:

\[
V_0 = 0
\]

The base's actual acceleration is also zero in the world. However, in RNEA one often initializes:

\[
A_0 = -g
\]

This is a **fictitious initialization** used to fold gravity into the acceleration recursion instead of adding gravitational wrench terms body-by-body.

So:

- physical fixed base: \(V_0 = 0\), actual \(A_0 = 0\)
- algorithm initialization: \(V_0 = 0\), recursive seed \(A_0 = -g\)

Both produce the same final gravity torques.

---

## 6. Local rigid-body dynamics: required body wrench

Once \(V_i\) and \(A_i\) are known, the required body wrench of link \(i\) is:

\[
F_i^{body} = G_i A_i + ad_{V_i}^*(G_i V_i)
\]

If an external wrench \(F_i^{ext}\) acts on the link, subtract it:

\[
F_i = G_i A_i + ad_{V_i}^*(G_i V_i) - F_i^{ext}
\]

Interpretation:

- \(G_i A_i\): inertial wrench from acceleration
- \(G_i V_i\): momentum
- \(ad_{V_i}^*(G_i V_i)\): bias wrench caused by differentiating momentum in a moving body frame

### Insight

This is the body-frame Newton–Euler equation. It is the moment where motion information is converted into required force information.

---

## 7. Backward pass: subtree wrench recursion

Let \(F_i^{\uparrow}\) denote the total wrench transmitted from the subtree rooted at body \(i\) to its parent.

Then:

\[
F_i^{\uparrow} = F_i + \sum_{j \in children(i)} Ad_{T_{i,j}}^* F_j^{\uparrow}
\]

Interpretation:

- the parent must support link \(i\)'s own wrench demand
- and also every child subtree's wrench demand, transformed into frame \(i\)

This is why the backward pass goes from leaves to root.

---

## 8. Joint torque extraction

The scalar actuator effort at joint \(i\) is the component of the transmitted wrench along the joint screw axis:

\[
\tau_i = S_i^T F_i^{\uparrow}
\]

This is a power-consistent projection:

\[
\tau_i \dot q_i = (F_i^{\uparrow})^T (S_i \dot q_i)
\]

So \(S_i\) selects the wrench component conjugate to the allowed joint motion.

---

## 9. Full RNEA algorithm in modern robotics language

### Forward pass

\[
V_i = Ad_{T_{i,\lambda(i)}} V_{\lambda(i)} + S_i \dot q_i
\]

\[
A_i = Ad_{T_{i,\lambda(i)}} A_{\lambda(i)} + S_i \ddot q_i + ad_{V_i}(S_i \dot q_i)
\]

\[
F_i = G_i A_i + ad_{V_i}^*(G_i V_i) - F_i^{ext}
\]

### Backward pass

\[
F_i^{\uparrow} = F_i + \sum_{j \in children(i)} Ad_{T_{i,j}}^* F_j^{\uparrow}
\]

\[
\tau_i = S_i^T F_i^{\uparrow}
\]

---

## 10. Two-link robot example

Consider a 2-link serial robot with link 1 as parent and link 2 as child.

We use the child-body frame convention.

### 10.1 Setup

Joint screw axes:

\[
S_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix},
\qquad
S_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}
\]

These represent revolute joints about the local \(z\)-axis.

Base initialization:

\[
V_0 = 0,
\qquad
A_0 = -g
\]

We denote transforms:

- \(T_{1,0}\): parent-to-child transform from base to link 1 frame
- \(T_{2,1}\): transform from link 1 frame to link 2 frame

### 10.2 Twist forward pass

For link 1:

\[
V_1 = Ad_{T_{1,0}} V_0 + S_1 \dot q_1 = S_1 \dot q_1
\]

For link 2:

\[
V_2 = Ad_{T_{2,1}} V_1 + S_2 \dot q_2
\]

Interpretation:

- link 1 has only its own joint twist, because the base is fixed
- link 2 inherits link 1 motion, re-expressed in frame 2, then adds its own joint twist

### 10.3 Acceleration forward pass

For link 1:

\[
A_1 = Ad_{T_{1,0}} A_0 + S_1 \ddot q_1 + ad_{V_1}(S_1 \dot q_1)
\]

Since \(V_1 = S_1 \dot q_1\), the last term is:

\[
ad_{V_1}(S_1 \dot q_1) = ad_{S_1 \dot q_1}(S_1 \dot q_1)=0
\]

because the Lie bracket of a twist with itself is zero.

So:

\[
A_1 = Ad_{T_{1,0}} A_0 + S_1 \ddot q_1
\]

For link 2:

\[
A_2 = Ad_{T_{2,1}} A_1 + S_2 \ddot q_2 + ad_{V_2}(S_2 \dot q_2)
\]

Now the convective term is generally nonzero, because link 2 sees a mix of inherited twist and its own joint twist.

### 10.4 Local body wrenches

Link 2 body wrench:

\[
F_2 = G_2 A_2 + ad_{V_2}^*(G_2 V_2) - F_2^{ext}
\]

Link 1 body wrench:

\[
F_1 = G_1 A_1 + ad_{V_1}^*(G_1 V_1) - F_1^{ext}
\]

### 10.5 Wrench backward pass

Link 2 is a leaf, so:

\[
F_2^{\uparrow} = F_2
\]

Joint 2 torque:

\[
\tau_2 = S_2^T F_2^{\uparrow} = S_2^T F_2
\]

Now propagate to link 1:

\[
F_1^{\uparrow} = F_1 + Ad_{T_{1,2}}^* F_2^{\uparrow}
\]

Joint 1 torque:

\[
\tau_1 = S_1^T F_1^{\uparrow}
\]

### 10.6 What this example shows

- The **forward pass** builds link motion recursively:
  - link 2 twist depends on link 1 twist
  - link 2 acceleration depends on link 1 acceleration plus a convective correction
- The **backward pass** builds subtree wrench recursively:
  - link 2 contributes to joint 2 torque directly
  - its required wrench is transformed and added to link 1's wrench
  - thus joint 1 must support both link 1 and link 2 dynamics

This is exactly why proximal joints usually carry more dynamic load.

---

## 11. Key insights

### Insight 1: motion forward, force backward

RNEA follows causality:

- motion is inherited from ancestors, so twists and accelerations propagate forward
- loads accumulate from descendants, so wrenches propagate backward

### Insight 2: body coordinates make inertia constant

Body spatial inertia \(G_i\) is constant in the body frame. This is a major reason the algorithm is efficient.

### Insight 3: nonlinear terms appear locally

Instead of one giant symbolic \(C(q,\dot q)\dot q\), RNEA encodes nonlinear rigid-body effects locally through:

- \(ad_{V_i}(S_i \dot q_i)\)
- \(ad_{V_i}^*(G_i V_i)\)

### Insight 4: joint torque is a projection, not the whole wrench

The propagated wrench \(F_i^{\uparrow}\) is a full 6D wrench. The actuator torque \(\tau_i\) is only its component along the joint screw axis.

## Appendix A. Deriving \(Ad\), \(Ad^*\), \(ad\), and \(ad^*\)

## A.1 Finite transform of twists: \(Ad_T\)

Let a rigid transform be:

\[
T = \begin{bmatrix} R & p \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}
\]

Suppose a body twist in one frame is:

\[
V = \begin{bmatrix} \omega \\ v \end{bmatrix}
\]

Under a rigid change of coordinates, angular velocity rotates as:

\[
\omega' = R\omega
\]

Linear velocity at the new origin becomes:

\[
v' = p \times (R\omega) + Rv
\]

Therefore:

\[
V' = Ad_T V
\]

with

\[
\boxed{
Ad_T =
\begin{bmatrix}
R & 0 \\
[p]_\times R & R
\end{bmatrix}
}
\]

This is the finite rigid transform rule for twists.

---

## A.2 Finite dual transform of wrenches: \(Ad_T^*\)

Twists and wrenches pair by power:

\[
F^T V
\]

We require power to be invariant under coordinate change:

\[
F^T V = (Ad_T^* F)^T (Ad_T V)
\]

for all \(F, V\).

This implies:

\[
\boxed{
Ad_T^* = (Ad_T^{-1})^T
}
\]

Carrying out the block-matrix calculation gives:

\[
\boxed{
Ad_T^* =
\begin{bmatrix}
R & [p]_\times R \\
0 & R
\end{bmatrix}
}
\]

up to transform-direction convention.

### Meaning

- \(Ad_T\): finite transport of twists
- \(Ad_T^*\): finite transport of wrenches

---

## A.3 Infinitesimal transform of twists: \(ad_v\)

Now consider a body moving instantaneously with twist:

\[
v = \begin{bmatrix} \omega \\ v_{lin} \end{bmatrix}
\]

The induced transport of another twist \(u\) is given by the Lie bracket:

\[
ad_v u = [v,u]
\]

In matrix form:

\[
\boxed{
ad_v =
\begin{bmatrix}
[\omega]_\times & 0 \\
[v_{lin}]_\times & [\omega]_\times
\end{bmatrix}
}
\]

so that

\[
ad_v u = v \times u
\]

This is the infinitesimal motion-side transport operator.

---

## A.4 Infinitesimal dual transform of wrenches: \(ad_v^*\)

We derive \(ad_v^*\) from the requirement that the motion-induced contributions do not create artificial power.

Let \(u\) be a twist and \(F\) be a wrench attached to a moving frame. Require the induced frame-motion contributions to satisfy:

\[
\frac{d}{dt}(F^T u) = 0
\]

for the pure transport part.

So:

\[
\dot F^T u + F^T \dot u = 0
\]

If the twist transport law is:

\[
\dot u = ad_v u
\]

and we define the wrench transport law by:

\[
\dot F = ad_v^* F
\]

then:

\[
(ad_v^* F)^T u + F^T(ad_v u) = 0
\]

for all \(F,u\).

Thus:

\[
\boxed{
(ad_v^* F)^T u = -F^T(ad_v u)
}
\]

Since this holds for all \(F,u\), we get:

\[
\boxed{
ad_v^* = -ad_v^T
}
\]

Now using the explicit matrix for \(ad_v\):

\[
ad_v =
\begin{bmatrix}
[\omega]_\times & 0 \\
[v_{lin}]_\times & [\omega]_\times
\end{bmatrix}
\]

and the identity \([a]_\times^T = -[a]_\times\), we obtain:

\[
\boxed{
ad_v^* =
\begin{bmatrix}
[\omega]_\times & [v_{lin}]_\times \\
0 & [\omega]_\times
\end{bmatrix}
}
\]

Therefore, for a wrench \(F = \begin{bmatrix} n \\ f \end{bmatrix}\):

\[
ad_v^* F =
\begin{bmatrix}
\omega \times n + v_{lin} \times f \\
\omega \times f
\end{bmatrix}
\]

This is the infinitesimal force-side transport operator.

---

## A.5 Relation between finite and infinitesimal operators

The lowercase operators are the infinitesimal versions of the uppercase ones.

Very roughly:

\[
Ad_{\exp(\hat v dt)} \approx I + ad_v dt
\]

and therefore

\[
Ad_{\exp(\hat v dt)}^* = (Ad_{\exp(\hat v dt)}^{-1})^T
\approx I - ad_v^T dt
\]

so:

\[
\boxed{
ad_v^* = -ad_v^T
}
\]

This is the clean bridge between:

- finite frame transforms: \(Ad, Ad^*\)
- instantaneous moving-frame transport: \(ad, ad^*\)

## Appendix B. Mapping back to Featherstone notation

| Modern Robotics language | Featherstone notation |
|---|---|
| body twist \(V_i\) | \(v_i\) |
| body acceleration \(A_i\) | \(a_i\) |
| body wrench \(F_i\) | \(f_i\) |
| body spatial inertia \(G_i\) | \(I_i\) |
| body screw axis \(S_i\) | \(S_i\) |
| parent index \(\lambda(i)\) | \(\lambda(i)\) |
| child set | \(\mu(i)\) |
| \(ad_{V_i}(\cdot)\) | \(v_i \times (\cdot)\) |
| \(ad_{V_i}^*(\cdot)\) | \(v_i \times^* (\cdot)\) |
| \(Ad_T\) | motion transform \(X\) |
| \(Ad_T^*\) | force transform \(X^*\) |

So the core RNEA equations become:

### Modern Robotics form

\[
V_i = Ad_{T_{i,\lambda(i)}} V_{\lambda(i)} + S_i \dot q_i
\]

\[
A_i = Ad_{T_{i,\lambda(i)}} A_{\lambda(i)} + S_i \ddot q_i + ad_{V_i}(S_i \dot q_i)
\]

\[
F_i = G_i A_i + ad_{V_i}^*(G_i V_i) - F_i^{ext}
\]

\[
F_i^{\uparrow} = F_i + \sum_{j \in children(i)} Ad_{T_{i,j}}^* F_j^{\uparrow}
\]

\[
\tau_i = S_i^T F_i^{\uparrow}
\]

### Featherstone form

\[
v_i = X_{i,\lambda(i)} v_{\lambda(i)} + S_i \dot q_i
\]

\[
a_i = X_{i,\lambda(i)} a_{\lambda(i)} + S_i \ddot q_i + v_i \times (S_i \dot q_i)
\]

\[
f_i = I_i a_i + v_i \times^* (I_i v_i) - f_i^{ext}
\]

\[
f_i^{\uparrow} = f_i + \sum_{j\in\mu(i)} X_{i,j}^* f_j^{\uparrow}
\]

\[
\tau_i = S_i^T f_i^{\uparrow}
\]

These are the same algorithm written in two notational styles.

---

## Closing remark

RNEA can be remembered in one sentence:

> propagate twists and accelerations forward, compute local body wrenches, propagate subtree wrenches backward, and project onto joint screw axes.
